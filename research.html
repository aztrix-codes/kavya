Research Themes

Theme 1: Groundwater Contamination

Overview

High concentrations of toxic geogenic elements—primarily uranium, fluoride, and arsenic—in drinking water systems pose severe health hazards to millions in India. This theme investigates the hydrogeochemical controls, spatial distribution, and release pathways of these toxic contaminants.

Key Questions

  • What environmental, geomorphological, and mineralogical variables control the release of contaminants into alluvial and crystalline aquifers?
  • Can we accurately predict pollution levels in unsampled geographical blocks?

Methods

We compile extensive groundwater chemical data and couple them with spatial predictive algorithms (e.g., Random Forest, XGBoost, Support Vector Machines) and interpretability libraries like SHAP (Shapley Additive exPlanations).

Datasets

Central Ground Water Board (CGWB) chemical logs, soil chemistry indices, gridded geological profiles, and meteorological records.

Outputs & Research Maps

High-resolution probability and hazard risk maps depicting groundwater contamination hotspots across selected states in India.

Theme 2: Geospatial AI & Machine Learning

Overview

Applying artificial intelligence and statistical learning to environmental remote sensing datasets. This work bridges the gap between raw planetary observations and physical hydrological outcomes.

Methods

Applying convolutional neural networks (CNNs), spatial neural networks, regression-kriging, and multi-sensor data fusion.

Applications

Hotspot predicting, model benchmarking under aquifer heterogeneity, and mapping spatial uncertainty boundaries.

Theme 3: Environmental Health & Exposure Assessment

Overview

Quantifying population-level exposure risks associated with environmental toxins.

Methods

Integrating population census maps, demographic dynamics, and contamination probability layers inside GIS environments to calculate regional hazard index (HI) values.

Applications

Estimating toxic ingestion dosages, locating vulnerable rural segments, and guiding public health monitoring efforts.

Theme 4: Policy Diagnostics & Governance Assessment

Overview

Investigating institutional frameworks and designing environmental governance diagnostics to improve municipal supplies.

Methods

Applying municipal water balance models, socio-economic survey surveys, and analyzing local policy mandates.

Applications

Feasibility audits of 24/7 continuous supply in metropolitan Delhi and mitigating light pollution in critical sanctuaries.

Theme 5: Cryosphere, Climate & Hydrology

Overview

Tracking rapid changes in High Mountain Asia's glaciers, seasonal snow cover, and permafrost distributions under changing climate cycles.

Methods

Mann-Kendall Trend test, Theil-Sen's slope analysis, and computing thermal indices (MAAT, PISR, BMAT) over 20-year MODIS time-series.

Applications

Mapping spatial permafrost boundaries in the Indus River Basin, tracking snow cover Dynamics, and charting elevation-wise LST changes.
