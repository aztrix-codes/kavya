Kavya Agrawal

Ph.D. Scholar

TERI School of Advanced Studies, New Delhi, India

I am a Ph.D. researcher in Natural & Applied Sciences. My work combines geospatial science, artificial intelligence, Earth observation, and hydro-environmental systems modelling to investigate groundwater contamination and environmental risk assessment across India.

My research integrates multi-source Earth observation datasets with machine learning and deep learning approaches to model groundwater contaminants (such as uranium, fluoride, and arsenic) and estimate human exposure risks across India. I work extensively with satellite-derived environmental layers, hydrogeochemical observations, climatic variables, and land-surface processes to build predictive and interpretable geospatial intelligence frameworks.
