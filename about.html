About Me

Academic Biography

My name is Kavya Agrawal, and I am a post-graduate researcher currently pursuing a Ph.D. in Natural & Applied Sciences at the TERI School of Advanced Studies, New Delhi, India. I hold a Master's degree in Geography from Dr. B.R. Ambedkar University, Agra, and a Bachelor of Arts (Honors) degree in Geography from Shaheed Bhagat Singh College, University of Delhi.

My research journey is rooted in geographical sciences and spatial reasoning. Over the course of my education, I developed an interest in investigating spatial distributions and physical anomalies. My work has evolved from local socio-economic impacts and urban sprawl analyses to modeling the dynamics of cold region hydrology, permafrost identification, and large-scale groundwater contamination assessment using cloud-computing tools and predictive modeling.

Currently, my Ph.D. research is focused on developing scalable and interpretable machine learning frameworks to model groundwater quality, focusing on toxic contaminants such as uranium, fluoride, and arsenic across India. By merging Earth observation datasets with hydrogeochemical profiles, I aim to create predictive tools that support long-term water safety.

Research Philosophy

I approach scientific research through an interdisciplinary lens. I believe that environmental problems—such as water scarcity, groundwater contamination, and cryospheric decline—cannot be solved in academic silos. Rather, they require a synthesis of physical hydrology, satellite observations, advanced data science, and governance diagnostics.

In my work, I emphasize:

  • Empirical Rigour: Utilizing multi-source Earth observation (MODIS, Landsat, Sentinel) combined with ground-truth water chemistry observations.
  • Interpretability in AI: Moving beyond "black-box" machine learning to build models whose predictions can be physically explained and verified by hydrologists and policy makers.
  • Actionable Science: Aligning models and spatial indicators directly with governance requirements to support sustainable and evidence-based environmental policy.

My long-term goal is to build transferable and scalable environmental intelligence systems that translate scientific models into local policies, contributing to climate resilience and public health.

Current Interests

My primary research interests span several overlapping domains in Earth systems and data science:

  • Groundwater Contamination Modeling
  • Geospatial AI & Deep Learning Applications
  • Environmental Exposure & Public Health Risk
  • Cloud-based Earth Observation (Google Earth Engine)
  • High Mountain Asia (HMA) Climate Dynamics
  • Permafrost Modeling & Spatial Extents
  • Glacier / Snow Cover Depletion Calculations
  • Water Security Governance & Policy Diagnostics

Future Directions

Moving forward, I intend to expand my research into:

  • National-Scale Risk Mapping: Building high-resolution, dynamic risk atlases for groundwater quality across the entire Indian subcontinent.
  • Climate-Induced Contamination Dynamics: Researching how shifting monsoon patterns, extreme climate events, and groundwater depletion affect pollutant concentrations over time.
  • Himalayan Glacier Runoff Modeling: Modeling how cryospheric melting changes downstream water availability and chemical exposures in major river basins.

I am keen to develop collaborations with international hydro-geologists, spatial statisticians, and public health researchers to co-design interdisciplinary frameworks.
